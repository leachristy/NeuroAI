Validation of Emotion and Activity Recognition using Physiological Signals
A Research Study Using BIOPAC Sensors & Emotiv Brainwave Headsets
Abstract: This study presents a comprehensive review and experimental validation of human and emotion activity recognition methods using physiological signals collected via wearable sensors, including the Senstream Research Ring, Emotiv EPOC X headset, B-Alert X10 headset and BIOPAC MP160 system. It summarizes common data collection methods, preprocessing techniques, extracted features, machine learning algorithms utilized, and overall performance. Furthermore, this research validates high-performing approaches with a newly collected dataset, proposing standardized methodologies for future comparisons. The findings highlight and emphasize the importance of standardized methodologies for reproducibility and comparability.

Comparative Analysis of the Effectiveness of Different Types of GANs in Improving Emotion Classification Based on Physiological Signals
A Research Study Using GAN-Based Data Augmentation and Wearable Biosensors
Abstract: This study investigates the application of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) for improving classification performance using physiological signals such as EEG, ECG, and PPG. We compare five GAN architectures—Vanilla GAN, DCGAN, WGAN, CGAN, and ACGAN—utilizing public datasets like SEED, MIT-BIH, and BIDMC, as well as proprietary data collected in our lab with devices including the Emotiv Epoch X, Biopac MP160 with RSPEC and PPGED modules, Senstream Research Ring, and B-Alert X10. Through GAN-based data augmentation, we address challenges like data scarcity and class imbalance, enhancing model robustness. Our findings indicate that WGAN and ACGAN outperform other models in generating high-quality synthetic signals, achieving superior classification results across all datasets. Despite issues like mode collapse and noise in some architectures, this work highlights the potential of GANs for advancing physiological signal classification.

EEG-Based Stress Detection and Personalized Music Recommendation System for Stress Relief
A Research Study Using EEG Signals and Machine Learning for Real-Time Stress Management
Abstract: This paper explores the integration of electroencephalography (EEG) / brain wave signals for stress detection and the efficacy of a personalized music recommendation system aimed at alleviating the detected stress in an individual. Using alpha and beta wave patterns derived from EEG, stress levels are assessed, while music recommendations are generated based on pre-trained user preferences. Machine learning models are used to predict Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) scores from collected EEG signals using Emotiv EEG headsets, and music selection was performed using Spotify. The results demonstrate the promise of EEG-based systems for real-time stress detection and stress response through dynamic music recommendations.
