Security Analysis of Distributed Virtual Reality and Physiological Sensor (VR-PHY) Systems
A Research Study on VR/AR Security, Neurophysiology, and Cybersecurity
Abstract: Virtual reality (VR) systems that are integrated with physiological sensors such as electroencephalography (EEG), electromyography (EMG), electrocardiogram (ECG) or galvanic skin response (GSR) provide immersive and interactive experiences in industries, including healthcare, education, and entertainment. Despite their increased usage, these systems face significant security challenges, particularly related to sensitive data interception, unauthorized access, and exploitation of the system. One of the primary components are VR headsets; they have unique vulnerabilities, including data leakage and susceptibility to malware. In this paper, we examine these security threats, focusing on data interception, and present mitigation strategies such as end-to-end encryption, biometric authentication, and anomaly detection to secure such interactive VR-PHY systems. As we examine the system architecture and known attack vectors utilizing a real-world implementation, this work highlights the importance of studying the security of these systems for reliable and safe user experiences.
